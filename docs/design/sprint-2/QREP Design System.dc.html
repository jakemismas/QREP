QREP design system · v1

The visual language for QREP, the quilt reverse-engineering platform. Warm craft-room materials, generous readable type, and honest uncertainty. Desktop-first, works down to an iPhone; every theme token ships in daylight and evening.

Sewing room, not software
Cream paper, stitched dividers, fabric swatches. No dashboards, no data slop.
Quilters’ arithmetic
Mixed fractions everywhere — 2½″, never 2.5. Finished sizes labeled as such; seam allowances spelled out.
Uncertainty is honest
Vision results show their confidence, always. Hand edits are certain — painting a square clears its doubt.

Color tokens

Fabric colors are the user’s and never theme; everything else swaps between daylight and evening.

Type

Bookman-family serif for headings and measurements; Seravek/Gill Sans for everything else. Body is 17px — quilters read glasses-off. Minimum 13px, and only for footnotes.

{{ t.label }} {{ t.sample }}

Shape & spacing

Radii. Cards 16 · panels & inputs 10 · segmented controls 11 outside, 8 inside · pills 999. Nothing sharp — everything slightly rounded like a well-washed quilt.
Spacing. Base 4px scale; cards pad 18–20; rows 12; section gaps 14–16. Hit targets ≥ 44px on touch, steppers 36–44px wide. Dashed dividers () mark section seams, like basting stitches.
Layout. Desktop: canvas + one 376px side panel with tabs. Tablet: same, panel narrows. iPhone: bottom tab bar (Quilt · Fabrics · Sizing · Pattern); panels become full-width pages.
